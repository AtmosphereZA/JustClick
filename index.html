JustClick OFFERS PERCEPTUAL STIMULATION WITH A TWIST!

JustClick uses technology (laptops and tablets) to develop perceptual skills in children to help them reach their full potential required for school readiness.

The outcomes will be:

  • Children that are intellectually, emotionally, and technologically well equipped
  • Children who will grow up to contribute positively to the social and economic prosperity of the community

50 different skills are continually developed and evaluated:

  • in a positive manner
  • in a relaxed atmosphere
  • in the known surroundings of their school
  • to ensure that minor or serious developmental problems are identified and addressed timeously e.g. poor concentration / low muscle tone/crossing of the midline
  • to encourage learners with a high learning capacity to develop a higher standard and to further their potential

About JustClick

For the past 20 years JustClick has been active in more than 45 schools and nursery schools in the east of Pretoria.

JustClick offers:

  • unique perceptual stimulation for pre-schoolers between the ages of three and six years, using laptops and tablets as educational tools
  • a new and exciting program every week (updated and changed annually)
  • individual attention in an encouraging and challenging atmosphere (only five learners per class)
  • age-appropriate software
  • uniquely prepared graded lesson plans and differentiated programs
  • weekly evaluation according to occupational therapist standards
  • early detection of possible problems e.g. poor concentration / low muscle tone / crossing the midline
  • three comprehensive reports per year
  • regular feedback to teachers
  • affordable fees

Our Mission

JustClick teachers are handpicked and passionate about their work. We strive to develop each toddler in a positive way by ensuring that each lesson is an unforgettable experience. We emphasise each child's uniqueness and individuality.

When necessary, support is provided by professional speech, occupational and play therapists.

What is S.T.E.M education?

STEM education stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. It emphasises the integration of these disciplines to provide a holistic and interdisciplinary approach to learning.

It promotes hands-on, experiential learning and encourages inquiry-based methods. Students engage in real-world problem-solving, fostering critical thinking and creativity.

Overall, STEM education not only develops Critical Thinking and Problem-Solving Skills, it provides a foundation for students to thrive in a technologically advanced and rapidly changing world.

When do Computers and tablets become detrimental?

  • when the above-mentioned are not adhered to and using the electronic device simply becomes a pleasurable pastime
  • when toddlers are allowed to repeatedly play the same games at home or even at school and they become intellectually and emotionally lazy
  • when toddlers spend more than 30 minutes per day on the computer or tablet

Important Note

To ensure whole-brain development, mental stimulation is very important during the first seven years of a child's life as all skills (intellectual, emotional, and technological) needed for mathematics, reading, and writing, are embedded during this period.

Key Terms:

Low Muscle Tone: Poor muscle tone affects posture, movement accuracy, and can lead to developmental delays, untidy handwriting, and concentration difficulties.

Crossing the Mid-line: The ability to reach across the body's center line, essential for writing patterns and motor skill development.
